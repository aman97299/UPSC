The Attorney General for India

The highest law officer in the country, as per Article 76 of the Constitution.

Appointment

Appointed by the President on the advice of the government.

Qualifications

Must be qualified to be a Supreme Court Judge.

Duties

Advises the Government on legal matters referred by the President.

Reference under Article 143

Represents the Government in matters referred by the President to the Supreme Court under Article 143.

Rights

Has the right of audience in all Indian courts.

Parliament

Can speak in and participate in parliamentary proceedings but cannot vote.

Privileges

Enjoys the same privileges and immunities as a Member of Parliament.

Term

Holds office "during the pleasure of the President."

Based on the Constitution of India, Article 76
